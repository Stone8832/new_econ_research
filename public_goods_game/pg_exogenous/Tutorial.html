You will play {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds. In each round you are placed into a firm (group) of size 2–6. Your firm stays the same for 10 rounds, then you are reshuffled into a new firm.

Your decision each round

You receive {{ C.ENDOWMENT }} effort units each round. You choose how many units to put into the firm. The rest you keep for yourself.

  • Effort kept for yourself earns 1 point per unit.
  • Firm effort is added up across your firm, and the firm’s payoff is shared equally among firm members (including you).
  • Your individual contribution is not shown to other players.

How the firm payoff works

Firm payoffs depend on your firm size. In some sessions, the firm payoff also increases more when the firm’s total effort is higher. Either way, higher total firm effort generally leads to higher firm payoffs.

Firm size Per-person return (α) per firm unit Total return (α × n)
2 0.65 1.30
3 0.55 1.65
4 0.49 1.96
5 0.45 2.24
6 0.42 2.50

Interpretation: each unit you contribute to the firm increases everyone’s payoff by about α points, and the group as a whole gains α×n points.

Timing

  • You have {{ C.DECISION_SECONDS }} seconds to decide each round.
  • If time runs out, your firm contribution is set to 0.
  • After each decision, there is a short info screen where you can see per-person outcomes by firm size.

Payments

You earn points during the experiment. Points are converted to cash at the end.
