You will play {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds. In each round, you first go through a firm formation stage. Unlike Treatments 1 and 2, firms are formed endogenously: you may apply to other firms, accept applicants into your own firm, or remain alone.

Your firm formation each round

  • You begin each round as the potential owner of your own firm.
  • You may apply to another participant’s firm by clicking their firm icon.
  • If your application is accepted, that acceptance is binding for the round.
  • If you accept an applicant into your firm, you are committed to operating that firm and may no longer apply elsewhere.
  • If you are accepted into another firm, your own firm becomes inactive for that round.
  • Applications still pending when firm formation ends are automatically rejected.
  • Firm size is capped at {{ C.MAX_FIRM_SIZE }}.

Autarky

If you are not employed by another firm and no one joins your own firm, you remain alone (autarky) for that round. In autarky, you simply keep your {{ C.ENDOWMENT }} effort units and earn {{ C.ENDOWMENT }} points.

Your decision each round

If you are in a firm with other members, you receive {{ C.ENDOWMENT }} effort units and choose how many units to put into the firm. The rest you keep for yourself.

  • Effort kept for yourself earns 1 point per unit.
  • Firm effort is added up across your firm, and the firm’s payoff is shared equally among firm members.
  • Your individual contribution is not shown directly to other players.

How the firm payoff works

In some sessions, the return per unit follows the constant-return schedule below:

Firm size Per-person return (α) per firm unit Total return (α × n)

In other sessions, the firm payoff has increasing returns to scale within the firm, so higher total firm effort raises total output disproportionately.

Resume information

During the firm formation stage, you can view every participant’s public resume before deciding where to apply or whom to hire.

  • To open a resume: click that participant’s player badge on the firm formation screen.
  • To apply to a firm: click the firm tile, not the player badge.
  • The resume helps you decide whether a participant looks like a good firm member or a risky hire.

A participant’s resume shows their history from prior rounds, including:

  • which firm they worked in,
  • who the members of that firm were,
  • firm size,
  • per-capita effort in that firm,
  • per-capita payout in that firm,
  • and whether they were terminated by a prior employer.

Termination information works as follows: if a participant worked for a firm in the previous round and that same employer rejects them in the next round while continuing to operate, that rejection appears on the participant’s resume as a termination. Ordinary failed applications do not all appear there; the resume highlights prior-employer terminations specifically.

Timing

  • You have {{ C.FORMATION_SECONDS }} seconds for firm formation.
  • You then have {{ C.DECISION_SECONDS }} seconds to choose your effort allocation.
  • If time runs out on the decision page, your firm contribution is set to 0.
  • After each round, there is a {{ C.INFO_SECONDS }}-second information screen.

Payments

You earn points during the experiment. Points are converted to cash at the end.
